Our Mission

Mission Statement:

Joanne established her CRO registered company, "Pure Drama Limited" alongside her husband, Clonakilty local and film-maker, Michael Downey. Together, the pair are on a mission to bring a whole range of activities to the town, so that the local tweens and teens can avail of opportunities that they would have loved to have had available to them when growing up in the town. With the ethos of the company centering around the motto, "There's something for everybody", they will strive to establish an inclusive, accessible place of joy that will cultivate the creative talents of the local community. Whether you want to perform professionally or simply to have a social outlet, Pure Drama will accommodate you.

What is hoped to be achieved:

  • A creative outlet that fosters the local talents
  • A two-hour long end-of-term show open to the public that will showcases the variety of talents developed over the term
  • A variety of classes offered to suit everybody

How will we achieve it:

  • The support and advertisement of local sponsons Clonakilty Secondary School and Dennehy's Supermarket
  • Access to the local community hall as rehearsal and performance space, as provided by the local council
  • The expert guidance of Joanne Downey who will be completely committed and available
  • Affordable and flexible payment plans that can be adjusted to the needs of the student

Perform, grow in confidence, be social!!

There's something for everyone!

Joanne Downey's Qualifications

Having developed a love for the stage whilst fulfilling her role as debating captain of 'Clonakilty Community School' through her tweens and teens, Joanne Downey knew a career in performance was for her. Without resources/classes at her disposal, Joanne worked tirelessly to keep informed on the Iirsh theatre scene and constantly refined her skills by learning new monologues and audition pieces. She decided on pursuing a B.A. Hons in 'Drama and Theatre Studies' from Trinity College, Dublin was was successful in her application. She specialised in Advanced Writing, receiving a first in this module and having her writing presented in the prestigious Samuel Beckett Theatre. During her time in Trinity, she also specialised in acting, directing and applied theatre. She was a very active member of the stduent society, where she wrote, directed and acted in a number of shows. Following her primary degree, she went on to complete an MFA in Playwriting in The Lir Academy. She was lucky enough to rub shoulders with excellent performers, creatives and deisgners from all over the globe, including the majority of the cast of Irish television series, "Normal People"

Joanne Downey's Experience

During her summers at universtiy, Joanne worked as the Head of Department at a Performing Arts Camp in Upstate New York, where she devised a programme for the campers that operated in tandem with the main camp (wherein six major musicals and plays were shown). Her curriculum was devised incorporating stand-comedy, improvisation, sketch comedy writing and drama (monologue/duologue performance) See a list of Joanne's writing and performing credits below:
